<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

[Gwibber](http://live.gnome.org/Gwibber), the microblogging client of
kings, is now [ready to
go](https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/jaunty-changes/2009-February/006022.html)
for Ubuntu 9.04. Ryan Paul has put up a
[Roadmap](http://live.gnome.org/Gwibber/Roadmap) of what’s to come.
Right now the [team](https://launchpad.net/~gwibber-committers) is
focused on bugfixing, bugfixing, and bugfixing. Thanks to Fabien Tassin,
Alexander Sack, and Sebastian Bacher for working to get this in in time
for Jaunty.

\[caption id=”attachment\_347” align=”alignleft” width=”145”
caption=”Gwibber”\]![Gwibber](http://castrojo.files.wordpress.com/2009/02/screenshot1.png?w=145)\[/caption\]
On a related note, noted pixel hero Jakub Steiner has published a
[kickass dark theme](http://jimmac.musichall.cz/log/?p=513) to go along
with it. A more conservative theme based on this is being worked on, of
course everyone involved would love to see that shipped by default.

I am happy to see Ryan’s code get into Ubuntu, for the longest time
gwibber was just a little sandbox for Ryan to mess around with whatever
new GNOME tech he wanted to play with. From there it grew into a real
project, with contributors to handle and bugs to fix. Adding
contributors has helped a bunch, it takes a certain “leap of faith” for
someone who hasn’t run an OSS project before to allow contributors to
mess around in your swampy mess and help you drain it. Gwibber is
finally to the point where there’s healthy non-Ryan people driving
development forward. It’s been a learning experience for me as well;
pushing out new revisions, doing bug work, and also learning tough
lessons (ie. webkit on hardy).

In a way watching gwibber grow to me has many parallels with how our own
Ubuntu community has been growing on microblogging services such as
[identi.ca](http://identi.ca), where we have grown to become the
[largest group](http://identi.ca/group/ubuntu) on the service. Cheers to
the core devs, Dominic Evans, Greg Grossmeier, Milo Casagrande, and Ryan
Paul. We certainly have a dedicated group of gwibber testers like
@technoviking, @gamerchick02, @popey, @gmb, , @maco, @myrtti, @jdub,
@jimmac, and who can forget @bugabundo. Of course thanks to @evan for
always supporting the project.

So, [get gwibber](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/gwibber), get on identi.ca,
and join [the group sensation](http://identi.ca/group/gwibber). Now, pep
talk over, let’s [get to work](https://bugs.edge.launchpad.net/gwibber).
;)
