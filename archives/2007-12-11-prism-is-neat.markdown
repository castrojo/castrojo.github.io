<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

Parts of [Mozilla Prism](http://labs.mozilla.com/2007/10/prism/) are
starting to hit Hardy. This is what the prism-google-talk one looks
like.

[![Gtalk](http://stompbox.typepad.com/blog/images/2007/12/11/gtalk.png)](http://stompbox.typepad.com/.shared/image.html?/photos/uncategorized/2007/12/11/gtalk.png)

It’s quite cool, you’d have a hard time realizing that this is a web
application, each of the apps has a launcher in the menu (Which I moved
to my panel). It even makes that “plunk” sound when someone IMs you.
I’ve decided to try an experiment and remove my gmail and gcal bookmarks
and just use the gmail and gcal “applications”.

I like that both the gmail and gcal prism app act like a local app.
There’s no way to open a new tab, or accidentally move to another url or
anything like that. In fact, when you click on a link in gmail it does
exactly what a fat app does, it just passes the URL to the browser.
Pretty cool.
