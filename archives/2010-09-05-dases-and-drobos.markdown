I need to plop on a few Terabytes at home.

I am looking for a [Direct Attached
Storage](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct-attached_storage) device. I
already have a home server, but due to lack of space in the case and the
fact that it’s doing a great job being an NFS and Samba box that I don’t
need a NAS. I’d like something I can just eSATA right to my existing
box.

I am about 85% sure that I need a [Drobo
S](http://www.drobo.com/products/drobo-s.php) to fill my needs. However
it is quite expensive, so before I decide to commit I want to ask
around. 

I know some people have built [alternatives to the
Drobo](http://frankleng.me/2010/05/01/zfs-powered-nas-the-ultimate-alternative-to-drobo-droboshare-the-complete-guide/),
but I’m not looking to replace my ubuntu-server OS (since it’s rocking)
or run a speciallized OS. I want a box I can just plug in and get
Drobo-like behavior. I want to be able to use drives I might have laying
around, and be able to just replace them when they die, and if the drive
that died is a small one I want to be able to plop in a larger one and
Just Work(tm).

From talking to people like Scott James Remnant and others at Debconf I
know it should be possible to build such a beast with btrfs that will do
what I want, the question is, how does one set this up? Ideally just add
on a dumb expansion bay with a bunch o’ drives that does what I
want. *Has anyone tried to make a drobo-like setup with btrfs yet?*
