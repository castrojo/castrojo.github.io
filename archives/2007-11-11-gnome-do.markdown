Every once in a while a bunch of random people tend to coalesce together
and come up with something cool. I discovered [GNOME
Do](https://edge.launchpad.net/gc) on accident in launchpad this week.
It’s a quicksilver/gnome-launch-box replacement we’ve all been
(hopefully) waiting for. David Siegel does a great job explaining it
[here](http://blog.davebsd.com/2007/10/30/do-gets-pidginevolution-integration/)
and [here](http://blog.davebsd.com/2007/11/09/we-got-the-jazz/).

The GNOME Do team has decided to leverage
[Launchpad](https://edge.launchpad.net/gc) for their needs, so this is a
general call for volunteers. Get in there and file bugs, translate, and
post questions/get answers. Keep in mind that GNOME Do is very much a
work in progress, but development is well underway, so this is the
perfect time to get involved. Their mailing list is
[here](http://groups.google.com/group/gnome-do?hl=en&lnk=li).

[Rick Harding](http://mitechie.com/) was kind enough to package GNOME Do
in his [Personal Package
Archive](https://edge.launchpad.net/~rharding/+archive), so feel free to
subscribe and get your debs from there.

I’m looking forward to seeing where this project goes.
