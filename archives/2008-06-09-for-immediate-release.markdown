Hockeytown - 9 July 2008 - The Ubuntu-Michigan Local Team, a leading
provider of Ubuntu Community today announced their ground breaking “Bug
Jam” event. Inspired but the Stanley Cup winning Detroit Red Wings, the
Local Team continues to pursue world-class level volunteership, peer
pressure, and group alcoholism.

“It’s going to be a lot of fun bringing people from around the community
and smashing bugs.” mentioned Local Team leader Greg Grossmeier. “We are
having a hard time finding out where the Stanley Cup is touring, so we
decided to lock ourselves up in a library and geek out instead. I mean,
seriously, we live in Detroit.”

“I honestly don’t have anything better to do with my time” mentioned
long time contributor Craig Maloney, “now that hockey is done for the
season I look forward to triaging bugs and cross-stitching.” Even the
local trolls were excited: “I look forward to disrupting the team and
talking about how great Visual Studio is…” remarked
‘that-guy-no-one-likes-but-fun-to-drink-with’, Jay Wren.

The team expects to participate in the [Global Bug
Jam](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/GlobalBugJam) in August, which has drawn
ire from competing organizations. “It’s really unfair, the league needs
some kind of ‘awesomeness cap’ or something, “ commented Richard Johnson
from the Chicago team, “we never get to think of fun things and I’m too
busy hanging out with the Foresight guys.” Members from the Ohio
community were also distraught. “I wish I was from Michigan, but I’m
not, I’m from freaking Ohio.” lamented Ohio native Steve Stalcup.

The Jam runs today, Monday June 9th at 6:30pm at the Main branch of the
Clinton-Macomb Public Library. Members will be in a conference room with
free wifi to library card holders. People without local library cards
can sign up for one in 5 minutes, but if you are planning on doing that,
please try to show up a little bit early. Directions can be found
[here](http://tinyurl.com/5rygg3).

The team plans on getting a bunch of practice in before the Global Bug
Jam … “Wow, that’s today?” remarked a surprised Trevor Jagoda.
