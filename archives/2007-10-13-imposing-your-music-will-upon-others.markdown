<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

The absolutely awesome [gajim](http://www.gajim.org) instant messenger
has a really great feature, you can set your IM status to show your
friends what songs you’re listening to. This is really great because
your friends can totally call you out when Abba comes up on random.
(Trust me, they won’t be kind).

Those of you who aren’t jabber-only, and depend on pidgin to talk to
your friends can look at
[musictracker](http://code.google.com/p/musictracker/), which is a
Pidgin plugin that accomplishes the same thing.

Unfortunately, musictracker is not in Ubuntu, but someone has intended
to package it [for
Debian](http://www.mail-archive.com/debian-wnpp@lists.debian.org/msg67900.html).
This means that it will eventually be in Ubuntu, but probably for 8.04.
An enterprising user can probably package it up in a
[PPA](https://help.launchpad.net/PPAQuickStart) for 7.10 (gutsy),
however.

PS: This post’s total purpose is to see who out there is willing to go
through the PPA process and make this a package. Leave feedback about
PPA in my comments.

PSS: If you’re having a hard time, Daniel Holbach had an [MOTU
Q&A](http://fridge.ubuntu.com/node/1161) session earlier today. If you
missed it, catch him during [Ubuntu Open
Week](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuOpenWeek), for lessons on how to
make Ubuntu packages.

PSSS: And after that, if you enjoyed packaging, consider joining our
[MOTU team](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU), who do this kind of thing for
fun. Well, not just for fun, we *do* let them be masters of the universe
and whatnot ….
