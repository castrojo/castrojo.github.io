<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

[Aaron Lake](http://metallikop.com/) and I have been messing with this
little tool called [Simplifymedia](http://simplifymedia.com/).
Basically, you friend a bunch of people, and then Simplify presents
their music to your player (Banshee and Rhythmbox are supported) with
their music as a DAAP source.

[](http://stompbox.typepad.com/.shared/image.html?/photos/uncategorized/2007/12/18/screenshotland_of_confusion_genesis.png)!\[Screenshotland\_of\_confusion\_genes*2\](http://stompbox.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/2007/12/18/screenshotland\_of\_confusion\_genes*2.png)

Aaron uses it so that he can listen to his music at home while he is at
work, that way he doesn’t have to carry his ipod with him or other
stuff, he can just share with himself over the internet. Aaron
discovered it while looking for something for his iPhone, and let me
know immediately that they were planning a Linux client, so we mailed in
and asked to test it. Now the [linux
client](http://www.simplifymedia.com/blog/?p=29) is available.

It’s not perfect, currently the UI depends on Wine and the arial MS font
- but one of the developers assures me that this is temporary. Hopefully
it will be popular enough to justify the developer time to make a native
UI. Of course, if it was open source then someone could do that work for
them (like a native Banshee plugin), but that’s another conversation.

There’s a [last.fm group](http://www.last.fm/group/SimplifyMedia)
available too if you want to join. Enjoy!
