I’m at FOSSCON in a workshop listening to Asheesh Laroia work with two
volunteers about how to make your first contributions to an open source
project. He’s built (along with others) a tool called
[OpenHatch](http://openhatch.org).

It’s basically designed to connect people who want to work on stuff with
projects. I’m a big fan of the “I want to help” button, and it’s ability
to suck in information from projects (like your Launchpad bugs) and
assign those to people who might be able to fix them. So if you like
Python and you tell OpenHatch you like Python, it will give you python
things to fix. Nice huh?

Here’s an [example page](http://openhatch.org/+projects/Gwibber) for
Gwibber. You can also find [bite-sized
opportunities](http://openhatch.org/search/?q=&toughness=bitesize) which
are small bugs for people to get involved. This is the kind of developer
&lt;-&gt; contributor relationship builder that would really rock for
projects. 

I hope Launchpad developers go check it out. :)
