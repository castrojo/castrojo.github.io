    Dodged a Bullet ....

    I was at BW3 today watching MSU advance in the NCAA Tourney when I got the frantic call from Jill … her Dell M1330 was booting up to a weird rainbow-esque screen. I knew right away that it was a hardware problem. Unfortunately she had a paper due this evening, and there was not much I could do from the bar. So I talked her down to my spare laptop in the basement and talked her into logging into my old hp 2510p laptop.

    When she started school I set her up with LyX on her windows machine. LyX is a really great piece of software, and when mated together with something like JabRef it’s really a great combo for academic papers. Spitting out high quality APA-style papers from LyX has been really working out great for her. Unfortunately for Jill, my spare laptop didn’t have LyX or anything like that installed, so when she called me I figured “Hey, she can just input text into OOo until the MSU game is over then I can come home …”

    Unfortunately for me, she got some error about Openoffice needing a JRE(!) to save her document, so I talked her through on how to launch gedit and paste the text in there and save it. Then when I came home I did what we must certainly expect all Ubuntu users to do, that is, install a JAVA RUNTIME ENVIRONMENT so that she can USE A WORD PROCESSOR. Anyway, after I installed the JRE, and pasted the text into OOo and the paper sort of got done. UPDATE: Chris Cheney tells me on irc that Java is needed for certain formats (like the MS XML ones) because the filters need xslt which is in Java or something. Unfortunately this isn’t installed by default because there’s no room on the CD for all this java stuff. So when in doubt, install the full openoffice packages)

    Formatting it was basically the worst 45 minutes of my life so I just went ahead and pasted the text into LyX to finish the project. The loose end was calling Dell support, it ends up with M1330’s that the nvidia cards tend to just stop working, so they’re sending a tech tomorrow to fix it on site.

    Lessons learned here:

    • Dell’s XPS support is pretty good. It sucks that the laptop broke, but the support was nice.

    • Learn raw LaTeX or use LyX, you’ll end up saving more time over using a word processor, especially OOo.

    • I know a guy that wrote an entire book in OOo. Yeah, seriously.

    • My hp 2510p (even though it’s a spare) has been a rock solid laptop, even though it’s on backup duty for me…

    • When given the option of making a girlfriend/spouse use OOo or coming home earlier from hanging out with your friends, pick the latter.

    • Using Dropbox saved us a bunch of work, I set up her laptop to save things in her dropbox folder by default, so she lost no work when her laptop blew up. Whew!

    By the way MSU 74, USC 69 …
