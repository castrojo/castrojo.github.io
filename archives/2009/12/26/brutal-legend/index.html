    Brutal Legend.

    Ok, so this game has been in my inbox for about 3 weeks (yay GameFly!), so I haven’t had a chance to play it until today. I’ve been distracted by Dragon Age: Origins - which is a separate post; some people have called it the greatest RPG of the decade. It’s up there, and probably, and yeah I am a Bioware addict, but I don’t want to go there right now.

    I want to talk about Brutal Legend.

    It’s pretty awesome, and here’s why (and why not!)

    • It’s metal - It’s got the right things. Ozzy, Lemmy, Megadeth, and a bunch of others. The derived things that they didn’t get licenses for are suitably mimicked. There’s one level where I swear I am getting bombarded by Dragonforce for like 45 minutes. At the end I was like “Jesus man, it’s like some kind of Dragonforce nightmare!” And then I found out it really was Dragonforce. Yay.

    • The playlist - It’s proper, and the bits you don’t agree with are part of the evil plot. See the part about Dragonforce!

    • The shitty playlist - Motley Crue, Ratt, Dokken, Quiet Riot, Whitesnake, and Skid Row. I thought this game was about metal?

    • FUCKING ANVIL

    • The playlist, really - I love the mix overall, I mean, you’ve got Testament in there, a bit of Slayer, some Priest, and even Dethklok, etc. I dig having the original Diamondhead “Am I Evil?” and Budgie’s “Breadfan” on there.

    • I got sick of the “play a concert” levels, with trying to get fans and stuff, mostly I just wanted to bash some skulls in.

    I enjoyed Jack Black in the game. At first I thought I would get a Jack Black overload, like in “Be Kind Rewind” where the whole movie sucked so you got stuck with Jack Black in full-on-mode for 2 hours and then you’re all pissed off at the end, but for those 20 minutes where did his gold parts acting out in the individual movies it was great. This is more of a “Jack Black in a supporting role” kind of thing, where you’re not fucking sick of him after 10 minutes. I like this kind of Jack Black.

    It’s obvious that the people that made this game listen to metal, I salute them, I literally laughed out loud plenty of times in this game. However there are things that you know “should be” in this game if it wasn’t for record companies and all that stupid crap or some kind of drama.

    • Thrash is not well represented - 1 Slayer song, 2 megadeth songs, 1 anthrax song, and 0 metallica, whoops!

    • NWOBHM - There is 0 Iron Maiden in this game. You made a game about heavy metal without any Iron Maiden, you get a prize!

    • Ozzybath - Plenty of Ozzy and Black Sabbath, this is really great!

    I suspect the music soundtrack is like the first Guitar Hero game, a bunch of artists and managers probably being conservative about whether they should be involved; I totally love that the game infers to managers and the music industry being … well, you know. I suspect that for the sequel we’ll get the proper metal bands that we want plus all the chaff, and in the end what the fans really will want is a combination of the soundtrack of the two games….

    Interesting wikipedia article with all the goodies. I like the game so far, if anything it’s damn clever, all the metal monuments across the land, the little injokes, the not-so-subtle stereotypes. I’ll keep playing it over the break, please leave your comments below!
