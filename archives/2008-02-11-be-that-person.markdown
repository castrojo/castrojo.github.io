Daniel Holbach recently blogged about [Ubuntu Developer
Week](http://daniel.holba.ch/blog/?p=84%22).

We have a [Full Week](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek) of
developer oriented programming coming up, and I just want to reiterate a
few things I’ve talked to about to people over the years:

-   You can do this.

-   Ubuntu is a community-run project, if you have ideas, and want to
    get involved, then this is the perfect “on-ramp” to get involved
    in development.

-   We have experts standing by: On top of this upcoming we week, we
    have regularly [scheduled
    events](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU/GettingStartedscheduled) where
    we answer questions and generally help develop your skills.

-   Our crack team of MOTUs have a list of [bitesize
    bugs](https://launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bugs?field.tag=bitesize), for
    those of you who are experienced enough to handle easy-to-fix bugs.

-   Debdiffs are an awesome way to fix those kind of bugs, catch Daniel
    [on Tuesday](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek) to learn
    how to make one.

-   Check out how to be involved in a [hug
    day](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuBugDay) to fix bugs

If you’re looking to get involved, then work off this list … I know that
[for sure](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=305) there are
people out there running hardy always wondering how to make Ubuntu
better, now is your chance; come get some.

And if you can’t do that, at least [digg
it](http://www.digg.com/linux_unix/First_ever_Ubuntu_Developer_Week_announced).
:)
