Well, it’s Friday, so what better way than to start the weekend than to
talk about patches!

I have been working on consolidating patch information from around the
wiki and talking to folks and have put all this information on this
[patches page](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDevelopment/Patches) in the
Ubuntu Development namespace.

This page is an amalgamation of tips I’ve been gleaming from experienced
Ubuntu developers and people in upstream projects who have been sending
me information. If you’ve got anything to add, please do so. There are
three main themes I’ve been hearing from different upstreams that seem
to be the common denominator when it comes to patches, so I thought I
would share them with you:

-   The willingness to rework/update a patch - By far the biggest
    attribute that upstreams seem to care about the most is the attitude
    of the person who is writing the patch. The willingness to rework
    the patch to follow guidelines or updates in the software and/or bug
    is a big plus. Also, ensuring your patch applies to trunk (and not
    just the branch you’re fixing) is important.

-   One patch per bug/feature - This seems pretty standard practice
    across the board.

-   Correctness - This is seemingly obvious, but I think it ties into
    point \#1, if you’re following point 1 then you should reach
    this eventually. :)

The rest of the tips are on the [patches
page](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDevelopment/Patches), if you have
any feedback please feel free to contact me directly or add to the page.
On occasion both Ubuntu and upstream developers drop me a note about a
certain patch/bug that is not being handled correctly, at which point we
do our best to resolve the issue.

For those of you writing patches and sending them upstream, I encourage
you to check out the [Patch Tagging
Guidelines](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDevelopment/PatchTaggingGuidelines),
which is a nifty way of bringing context into a patch. These are
starting to become popular with some teams (The Security Team are now
using them as part of their team policy). It’s pretty easy, just a
comment that links to both the Ubuntu and upstream bug tracker. When an
upstream sees this patch it helps give them *context* on why the patch
exists and our justification for doing it.

As always, please give me feedback as you use these resources, or if
you’re not using them, tell me why!
