<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

Jono’s blogged about [what we’re
doing](http://www.jonobacon.org/2010/11/20/new-ubuntu-patch-pilot-scheme/) to
keep the sponsorship queue healthy. 

It can be [a real
bummer](http://castrojo.tumblr.com/post/1582239180/make-it-really-easy-to-fix-bugs-on-ubuntu#comment-97638260) when
contributions are ignored so I am glad we’re taking a more proactive
stance on the problem and setting aside time for people to do it. You
can find out more about the [Sponsorship Process
here](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SponsorshipProcess).

Another important element to accepting gifts is [Operation
Cleansweep](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/OperationCleansweep). Here’s the
stats for the week:

    Total bugs with patches: 2395 (+10)
    Reviewed patches: 428 (+1)
    ---
    Bugs with 'patch-needswork': 99 (-1)
    Bugs with 'patch-forwarded-upstream': 192 (+5)
    Bugs with 'patch-forwarded-debian': 63 (+1)
    Bugs with 'indicator-application': 38 (0)
    Bugs with 'patch-accepted-upstream': 60 (-1)
    Bugs with 'patch-accepted-debian': 10 (0)
    Bugs with 'patch-rejected-upstream': 19 (0)
    Bugs with 'patch-rejected-debian': 3 (0)
    ---
    Last updated: Sun, 21 Nov 2010 08:05:42 +0100

Plenty of work for many more people! If you want to dive in hit the
[Getting
Involved](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ReviewersTeam/GettingInvolved) page –
Cleansweep is a good place to get started, you just need to know how to
review code, you don’t have to worry about learning all the Ubuntu
Developer-specific workflow to contribute!
