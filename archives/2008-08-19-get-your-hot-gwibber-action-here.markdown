\[![Screenshotgwibber\_2\](http://stompbox.typepad.com/blog/images/2008/08/19/screenshotgwibber\_2.png)](http://stompbox.typepad.com/photos/uncategorized/2008/08/19/screenshotgwibber_2.png)Ok,
so when [Ryan Paul](http://arstechnica.com/authors.ars/segphault) (from
Ars Technica) started working on gwibber as a twitter client I was
mildly interested. It wasn’t until [identi.ca](http://identi.ca) was
launched that I moved wholesale and needed a good client. (Twhirl and
Spaz are good too but the Linux Adobe AIR alpha is holding them back).

[gwibber](http://launchpad.net/gwibber) was kind of Ryan’s playground
application where he could mess around with things like webkit and GTK
stuff. However, people wanted an identi.ca client, and started using it.
Ryan wanted to do the packaging himself to learn it (and hopefully write
it up for open.ended) so we specifically avoided packaging it. Well, it
became more and more popular, and it was time to make packages, it was
already in bzr and on launchpad, so it would be heresy to not leverage
the awesome power of Personal Package Archives.

This evening I registered the gwibber-team and set up a PPA.
Instructions are [here](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/gwibber). Note that it
depends on the webkit PPA, so you need both sets of sources. gwibber
uses webkit to make the cool layout you see in the screenshot, complete
with reflections and rounded corners!

Before anyone asks, no, I won’t be putting these up for Intrepid proper,
gwibber is still fast moving and needs work. Branches are [popping
up](https://code.edge.launchpad.net/gwibber) all over the place and
people are Doing Great Things(tm), but it’s just too fast moving to
maintain in the distro, which is why we have PPAs! With webkit coming in
a future version of GNOME it should start to settle down. You’ll find my
packaging branch at the previous URL, if you want to jump in and help
out then let me know!

You can follow me on identi.ca using gwibber, I am
[@jorge](http://identi.ca/jorge), and you can follow Ryan too,
[@segphault](http://identi.ca/segphault). Come join the other bunches of
Ubuntu users on identi.ca!

Installation notes for our openSUSE friends are
[here](http://zonker.opensuse.org/2008/08/19/gwibber-rocks/). If someone
wants to throw these into Debian post-freeze then please let me know,
and of course feel free to steal my packages!
