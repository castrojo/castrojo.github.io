I’d like to extend thanks to the many people out there who maintain the
Ubuntu wiki and forum threads; all of you excellent documentation folks.
I was easily able to look [at a
grid](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SkypeWebCams) of compatible webcams and
make a purchasing decision. Of course a thanks also go out to the folks
who actually wrote the driver. :)

I bought a Logitech Quickcam STX and it worked right out of the box.
Ekiga worked (once I set it to VL2). Cheese worked with zero
configuration , what an **excellent** application by the way; green hulk
angry!

[![](http://lh3.google.com/jorge.castro/R75Hkmca0tI/AAAAAAAAEbE/Se03zjSQgvo/s144/Screenshot-Cheese.png)](http://picasaweb.google.com/jorge.castro/RandomCrap/photo#5169648116388516562)

… and Skype (an unfortunate non-free necessity) just worked; spent some
time hanging out with Ubuntu Studio’s *MMA*:

[![](http://lh6.google.com/jorge.castro/R75HkWca0sI/AAAAAAAAEa8/0i_Z88tyhR8/s144/Screenshot-02%3A41%20%7C%20Call%20with%20addictedtometal.png)](http://picasaweb.google.com/jorge.castro/RandomCrap/photo#5169648112093549250)

The nice thing I liked about this camera is that it Just Worked. It came
with a CD, but I didn’t need it, i just plugged it in. Awesome.

On the other side of the coin, I went to micro center yesterday and got
a cheap USB stick. The box said nothing about the bundled [U3
software](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U3) that was installed on the
key, otherwise I would have known better. The only way to remove it is
with a windows-only tool. The weird part is that the the software
actually makes the usb stick harder to use, even in Windows. It’s like
someone somewhere said “Let’s not only invest money in this software,
but let’s make it harder for people to use as well, that way we all
win!”. I would have paid money to be at that meeting.

It’s either ironic (or sad) that these days the simplest devices are
restricted with worthless crap, while the seemingly more complicated
devices are Just Working(tm) these days. Well, except for [home
NAS](http://stompbox.typepad.com/blog/2007/12/home-nas-soluti.html)
stuff, heh.

So, the Logitech Quickcam STX gets a thumbs up, most USB sticks these
days get a thumbs down.
