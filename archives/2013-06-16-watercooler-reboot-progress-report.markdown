<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

Last month I tossed out the idea that Ubuntu as a project needs to
[fight to preserve our
watercooler](http://www.jorgecastro.org/2013/05/31/reinvigorate-the-water-cooler/).
The past 2 weeks have been a blur of progress and hard work by a bunch
of people, and I’d like to start posting regular reports on what we’re
working on.

The first step was deploying [Discourse](http://discourse.org). But we
didn’t want a one off deployment. We wanted to do this in a repeatable
way. A modern way that allows us to scale and grow that gives us the
ability to keep up with upstream’s [blistering
pace](https://github.com/discourse/discourse/commits/master). And
besides, if we’re going to make this easy to deploy, we should of course
share that devops expertise with the community, so here it is.

-   Here is the [Juju Charm for
    Discourse](http://jujucharms.com/~marcoceppi/precise/discourse) with
    instructions if you’re interested in your own.
-   [Discourse/Juju charm operational lessons
    learned](http://test.ubuntu-discourse.org/t/discourse-juju-charm-operational-lessons-learned/17) -
    these are our deployment notes.
-   and [how to contribute
    code](http://test.ubuntu-discourse.org/t/i-want-to-contribute-to-this-sites-code-how-do-i-do-that/275/4)
-   All deployed on performant instances on [HP
    Cloud](http://www.hpcloud.com), powered by OpenStack.
-   And here’s a great video from the Discourse guys on [how to run
    it](https://www.youtube.com/watch?feature=player_embedded&v=RRuU9i6r6FY)
    on a single server.

If you really want to play I recommend following the video instructions
from the Discourse guys, and then I want you to try the Juju charm – you
will then realize why I just can’t shut up about Juju; automate all of
that devops goodness so you don’t have to do it by hand every single
time. We got the site up and running, with Marco doing [regular
reports](http://test.ubuntu-discourse.org/t/mondays-and-thursdays-are-maintenance-days/172/12)
of the maintenance windows.

Next up was to make it pretty. Enter Nathan Osman, one of my favorite
get-things-done hackers in our community. He did most of the work and we
even got some pointers from the design team.

![](http://www.jorgecastro.org/images/ubuntu-discourse.png)

Not bad!

So how can you help us kick the tires on Discourse? Well, you can start
by using the site!

[ubuntu-discourse.org](http://ubuntu-discourse.org) {#ubuntu-discourseorghttpubuntu-discourseorg}
---------------------------------------------------

-   tell a friend.
-   Log in via your Ubuntu SSO. Or Google, or Yahoo or make a local one.
-   You can [integrate Discourse comments into your WordPress
    blog](http://test.ubuntu-discourse.org/t/anyone-with-a-wordpress-blog-want-to-give-this-integration-plugin-a-shot/321?u=jorge) -
    This plugin is exciting, it allows bloggers to integrate directly
    with the forum for a nice integrated effect. The comments get posted
    on Discourse instead, and then the highest liked ones get synced
    BACK to your blog.
-   Start posting interesting content! If it’s something you were going
    to blog or send to the list, consider posting it here. The nice
    thing is is that Discourse supports HTML, Markdown, and BBCode, so
    posting isn’t difficult.
-   [Give us
    feedback](http://test.ubuntu-discourse.org/category/feedback) so we
    can improve things.

And a special shoutout to the upstream developers for participating in
our test and steering us in the right direction.

So there it is, let’s see where this goes!
