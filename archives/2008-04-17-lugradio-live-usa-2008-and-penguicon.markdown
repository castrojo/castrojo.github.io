Whew, what a busy month it’s been so far.

I spent last weekend at [Lugradio Live
USA](http://lugradio.org/live/USA2008/) where I got to meet a bunch of
great people. I am convinced this show is going to expand a great deal.
I think it’s excellent to have community-driven shows, since ours are a
great deal smaller than the ones in Europe and the rest of the world.
Penguicon 6.0 is this weekend in Detroit. Check out the plethora of
[events](http://penguicon.org/events.php).

-   More [Lugradio Live coverage](http://automorphic.blogspot.com/) from
    Sandy Armstrong.

-   Other great community shows in the US are
    [SCALE](http://www.socallinuxexpo.org/),
    [Penguicon](http://penguicon.org/), [Ohio
    LinuxFest](http://ohiolinux.org/), and the new kid on the block,
    [Ontario Linux Fest](http://onlinux.ca/). I spent some time talking
    to one of Ontario Linux Fest’s organizers, Richard Weait, it’s going
    to be a great show this year, so stay tuned.

-   Don’t miss Scott James Remnant’s [updates on
    Upstart](http://www.netsplit.com/category/tech/upstart/).

-   Bored? Help test the Ubuntu 8.04 [release
    candidate](http://blog.qa.ubuntu.com/node/4) images.

-   After all that testing, reward yourself by attending one of the many
    [release parties](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardyReleaseParties) in an
    area near you. If there isn’t one, make one!

-   Check out Aaron and Gabriel’s talk about
    [Banshee](http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=629829973972985803&hl=en)
    from LRL-USA.

-   Thanks to [Zonker](http://zonker.opensuse.org/) and Novell for
    taking care of shipping the GNOME North American Event Box back to
    the Foundation.

-   Thanks to [Nathan Haines](https://edge.launchpad.net/~nhaines) and
    his whole family from the California LoCo for taking care of the
    Ubuntu Booth at LRL and making that rock.

-   Shout out to Dave, Vlad, and Chris from MoCo, Ian and Stephan
    from Songbird. Ian has an old Eazel shirt, I wonder how many people
    remember Eazel.

-   [Review Board](http://review-board.org/) is quite cool.

-   This is getting too long, if I missed you, post a comment. :)

Edit: Duh, of course, thanks to Aq, Matt, and Chris, whom I’ve never met
before, for being real life versions of their radio selves.
