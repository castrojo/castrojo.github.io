Sometimes I wonder, given all the things that have ever lived, what
would scare me the most?

In today’s world we have Orcas and white sharks in the water, lions and
tigers on land. And though not really scary, you probably don’t want to
mess with a [Cape Buffalo](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_buffalo).
There are definately extinct species that can really scare you, any kind
of [Tyrannosaurus](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyrannosaurus) or
[Spinosaurs](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spinosaurus) are scary. If you
don’t want to be eaten by one large dinosaur then being mauled by a
bunch of [large pack hunting
chickens](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deinonychus) doesn’t sound like a
particularly fun way to go either.

But there’s one thing that just frightens the hell out of me,
[Dunkleosteus](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dunkleosteus). A large bony
fish from the Devonian (\~370mill years ago), this guy is the honey
badger of extinct fish; first off, look at this skull:

![](http://www.jorgecastro.org/images/dunkleosteus_terrelli.jpg)

[Norwood Matt](http://www.flickr.com/photos/norwoodmatt/130704438/)

![](http://www.jorgecastro.org/images/640px-Dunkleosteus_interm1DB.jpg)

(Wikipedia)

This thing is just a swimming nightmare, I mean, come on, ya gotta be
kidding …

![](http://www.jorgecastro.org/images/Dunkleosteus.jpg)

I even found this (uncredited) pic, not my idea of a good time:

![](http://www.jorgecastro.org/images/paleo_day95.jpg)

So basically you take an Alien’s head from *Alien* and attach it to a
fish. It doesn’t even have teeth. Those “teeth” you see are actually
extensions of it’s skull; it’s basically skull teeth.

![](http://www.jorgecastro.org/images/130614_v2.jpg)
