[![](http://castrojo.files.wordpress.com/2010/03/3188694885_fbd048c3d5.jpg)](http://castrojo.files.wordpress.com/2010/03/3188694885_fbd048c3d5.jpg)
Photo by Germán Póo-Caamaño

The GNOME Foundation is always looking for support to help grow the
desktop we all love. The generous contributions of GNOME’s [Advisory
Board](http://foundation.gnome.org/about/) are always appreciated.
However a good portion of these funds come from people like you and me,
who want to support GNOME and see it prosper. Some people don’t know
that you too can support GNOME with donations. So where does that money
go? Here are some of the events we’d like to support this year that
would make a significant impact on improving GNOME:

-   [Marketing
    Hackfest](http://live.gnome.org/Hackfests/Marketing-2010-05) - This
    hackfest is primarily to define and being the GNOME 3.0
    launch plans.

-   [PyGTK Hackfest](http://live.gnome.org/Hackfests/Python2010) - Will
    take place in Cambridge, MA, 14th-18th April 2010, thanks to OLPC
    for hosting.

-   [GSettings
    Hackfest](http://live.gnome.org/Hackfests/GSettings2010) - Novell is
    hosting this April 12-17 in Cambridge, Boston, USA; thanks guys!

-   [GNOME Outreach Program for
    Women](http://projects.gnome.org/outreach/women/) - This one isn’t a
    hackfest but a sponsorship for individual interns.

You can do one time donations or a small monthly donation, whichever
works for best for you. Rock on.

**[Become a friend here](http://www.gnome.org/friends/).**
