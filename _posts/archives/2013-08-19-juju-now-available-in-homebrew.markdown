Thanks to Rodrigo Chacon OSX users can now install Juju via
[Homebrew](http://brew.sh/):

    brew install juju

And then deploy to your heart’s content. Figuring out Juju? Well, as you
learn you should think about submitting to the [Juju Charm
Championship](http://www.jorgecastro.org/2013/07/01/share-your-infrastructure-win-a-prize/),
where we’re giving away over \$30k in prizes to people/organizations who
want to share their infrastructure expertise with the community.

What is Juju? Juju is an orchestration tool to get your services and
code from your laptop to your cloud as fast and easy as possible. We’ve
got over [130
services](https://jujucharms.com/fullscreen/search/?series=precise&text=&type=approved)
ready for you to deploy either via the CLI tool or the Juju GUI, which
you can deploy on your own cloud too. We have common infrastructure
charms, or just deploy right from github via our
[Rack](http://jujucharms.com/charms/precise/rack) and
[node.js](http://jujucharms.com/charms/precise/node-app) charms.

Juju also deploys what we call “micro clouds” on your laptop via LXC
containers – this allows you to test your application on your laptop
just as if it was deployed on AWS/HP Cloud/OpenStack.

-   [juju.ubuntu.com/docs/getting-started.html](https://juju.ubuntu.com/docs/getting-started.html)
    and skip right to the configuration part to fire off your cloud.

Here’s a [small deployment of
Wordpress](http://www.jorgecastro.org/2013/07/31/deploying-wordpress-to-the-cloud-with-juju/)
on a single node, or you can check out [how to deploy
OpenStack](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mspwQfoYQks) graphically via
the Juju GUI.
