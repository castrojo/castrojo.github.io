<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

Kyle Rankin has recently updated his [Knoppix
Hacks](http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/9780596514938/)
([amazon](http://www.amazon.com/Knoppix-Hacks-Hacking-Repairing-Enjoying/dp/059651493X/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1196054226&sr=8-1))
book.

I can’t believe it’s been nearly three years since the first edition.
This book has saved my sysadmin butt many times, as well as helped me
recover data from people’s busted Windows installs. Kyle has also
authored [other excellent
books](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/search-handle-url/102-0216405-3561717?%5Fencoding=UTF8&search-type=ss&index=books&field-author=Kyle%20Rankin),
like Ubuntu Hacks and Linux Multimedia Hacks. Out of all of his books
though, Knoppix Hacks is his “magnum opus”. This book has been immensely
popular, by local LUGgers, the arslinux crew, and generally most people
I’ve ever met. It’s just really good.

When I do talks about Ubuntu, I always recommend two books: the
[Official Ubuntu
Book](http://www.amazon.com/Official-Ubuntu-Book-2nd/dp/0132354136/ref=pd_ts_b_1?ie=UTF8&s=books)
and [Ubuntu
Hacks](http://www.amazon.com/Ubuntu-Hacks-Tools-Exploring-Tuning/dp/0596527209/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1196054398&sr=1-1).
For about US\$45 you get an Ubuntu CD (EDIT: I mean DVD!) and a one-two
punch of intro and advanced topic books, for about half the cost of a
proprietary OS. It’s a pretty compelling value. What makes these two
books compelling is that they’re written by normal people, not the
typical “Crank out a linux book” series that are so common these days.

Those of you close to Michigan can catch Kyle at the next
[Penguicon](http://penguicon.org/) in April (along with Offical Ubuntu
Book co-authors Jono Bacon and Benjamin Mako Hill).

For fun, those of you who know both Kyle and me know that he secretly
includes an easter egg in every book about me. These commonly start with
“I have a friend who did this …” and usually ends up in something going
catastrophically wrong with your PC. It could be that my purpose in life
is to serve as a warning to others …
