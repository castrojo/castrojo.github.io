<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

Two very cool Juju stories this week. First off it’s Gustavo Neiemeyer’s
[The Heart of Juju](http://blog.labix.org/2013/06/25/the-heart-of-juju).
If you’re not really familiar with Juju and are confused on what it can
do for you then you’ll want to check this article out.

The second article is more for you experienced devops types. Michael
Nelsosn has outlined [Easier juju charms with Python
helpers](http://micknelson.wordpress.com/2013/06/24/easier-juju-charms-with-python-helpers/)
starting by adding support for declaring saltstack states in yaml.

One article for beginners, and one for you advanced users.

And for those of you like Alan Bell yelling “But wait Castro! I have no
cloud, I need Juju to scale down” … here’s a little bit of status for
you:

-   Containerization is [moving along
    well](https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/juju-dev/2013-June/001126.html) -
    this will give us the ability to run in containers in the
    deployed instances. Once we have this we can do two things people
    like Alan have been waiting for: the ability to run more than one
    service on an instance, and this will let us bring back the
    local provider.
-   This along with the ssh provider should enable anyone to run Juju
    charms on whatever machine has OpenSSH. Not bad.

So Alan, I expect to have you Juju enabled by the end of the summer,
13.10 tops.
