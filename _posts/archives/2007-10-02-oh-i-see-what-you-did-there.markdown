After something like a 9 month vacation from open source I’ve started
working for Canonical Ltd. on Ubuntu on Upstream Developer Relations.

The short explanation is that I’ll be working hand in hand with our
upstream friends who provide us the awesome software that we bundle in
Ubuntu. (GNOME, KDE, Debian, Xorg, etc.)

The long explanation is that we ship lots of software and I’m new at
this, so I’m keen on getting my workflows sane so I can start to work
with as many upstream projects as I can.

Our first big push to foster better relations with upstream is by
sponsoring [FOSSCamp](http://www.fosscamp.org), which is 27-28 October
in Cambridge, USA. There is already interest from upstreams, as you can
see from the [list of attendees](http://www.fosscamp.org/HowToAttend),
but we could always use more. The event is not-Ubuntu specific and open
to anyone who wants to rock, so if you’re involved in an upstream
project and want to stop by, then please sign up.

I’ve a long list of things to blog about, especially after attending
Ohio LinuxFest 2007, so I will be posting regularly to catch up.

Many many thanks to Oliver Grawert for twisting my arm, Jono Bacon and
Daniel Holbach for putting me on their team, and [Luke
Kanies](http://www.madstop.com/) from
[Puppet](http://reductivelabs.com/trac/puppet) for motivational head
punching.

Now enough of that … it’s time to get to work!
