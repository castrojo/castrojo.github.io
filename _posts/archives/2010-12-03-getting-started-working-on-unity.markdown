<div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">

Now that Alpha 1 is out the door the different bits are starting to come
together. We’ve got the compiz-based Unity in people’s hands, now it’s
time to start the feature work and testing.

The Desktop Experience team is building the base; but there are plenty
of other parts that need to be done. Keep in mind that these parts are
just as important and a critical piece of the entire user experience.

![](http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3306/3237202375_b6fa401530_m.jpg)

([Scott Mitchell](http://www.flickr.com/photos/esmtll/3237202375/))

When you pick up a Fender Geddy Lee Jazz Bass everything is as It Should
Be(tm). The attention to detail is evident in every part of the bass. No
one wants a cool looking instrument that sounds like slop, and no one
wants an instrument that sounds amazing but can’t stay in tune. The
person who does the seemingly unimportant part of polishing the neck
takes their job as seriously as the person choosing the wood, or the
person wiring the pickups. When you add all that up you get something
magical. So what does this have to do with Ubuntu?

For developers we’re kicking off a [Bitesize
Bug](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Unity/Bitesize) campaign. Over the next few
months we will be specifically finding [small, easy to digest
bugs](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bugs?field.tag=bitesize) that
are just as important to the experience as the person doing the
plumbing. This list will continue to grow and will contain not only
fixes, but feature work as well, now’s the time to get involved if you
want to get involved. Here’s a sample of some of the type of bugs we’re
looking at:

-   Bug 677577 - [Clicking on a launcher icon does not raise most recent
    window](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/677577)

-   Bug 646740 - [indicators are
    mis-aligned](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/646740)

-   Bug 683241 - [Recycle bin icon is empty when there are items in the
    bin](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/683241)

-   Bug 683466 - [There are 2 Quit menu options in
    Quicklist](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/683466)

-   Bug 599716 - [having “unity –replace” would be
    nice](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/599716)

-   Bug 681428 - [scrolling does not work on the sound
    menu](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/681428)

So how can people fix these?

1.  Run Natty. You can either do this by running Alpha 1 on your bare
    metal or [creating a USB
    key](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Unity/InstallUSBKey) for this purpose.
    I’ve added documentation to that page so you can install a
    development environment on it so you can hack on code and keep the
    entire updated Unity environment on the key itself.

2.  Pick a bug from [the
    list](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bugs?field.tag=bitesize).

3.  Follow [these instructions](http://unity.ubuntu.com/getinvolved/).

4.  Your work will be reviewed by someone on the DX team, after they
    review your code they will instruction you on what to do next.

5.  Celebrate!

6.  Go to step 1.

![](http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4017/5079758778_61d2c3969c_m.jpg)

Expect regular weekly progress reports from me on the rock star
craftspeople working on these bugs. The list of bugs will continually be
updated, and now that Alpha 1 is out the door expect a nice steady
stream of bitesize bugs that people can contribute fixes to.

Need help? Find us on \#ayatana on Freenode, or the [ayatana-dev mailing
list](https://launchpad.net/~ayatana-dev).
