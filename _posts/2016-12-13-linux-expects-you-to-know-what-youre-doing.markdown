There's a [thread on reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/Ubuntu/comments/5i2ggz/i_think_ubuntu_is_still_not_ready_for_beginners/) on why Ubuntu is still not ready for beginners.

Basically, an upgrade broke for someone's family member, and the poster had to go and fix it, and that's really crappy. He was asking about how we're going to fix it. Some of the comments were just incredible to me. 

> Maybe they should stop aborting upgrades.

We've become so used to this mentality that we think it's people's fault for aborting an upgrade. I've got a better idea for you computer, _don't break_.  

Would you buy a Chromebook if it came with a sticker that said "Warning, don't close lid when doing maintenance, if you don't know this then you're supposed to know what you're doing, please learn Linux." 

> Linux was NEVER intended to be a training wheels OS.

And yet people use Android and ChromeOS machines every day without training wheels. The reality is that it's 2016 and transactional updates are a requirement if Ubuntu is to remain competitive.
